Detailed Description

template<int dim>
class Forest::MomentIndexer< dim >

Indexes spherical harmonics moments.

The one group (or within-group) scattering source in 3-d is defined

\[ Q(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{\Omega}) = \sum^L_{l=0} \frac{2l+1}{4\pi} \sum^l_{m=-l} \Sigma_{sl}(\mathbf{r}) \phi^m_l(\mathbf{r}) . \]

and for the representation of the angular flux in Spherical Harmonics

\[ \Psi(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{\Omega}) = \sum^L_{l=0} \sum^l_{m=-l} Y_{l}^{m}(\mathbf{r}) \Phi^m_l(\mathbf{r}) . \]

Such sums over \(l\) and \(m\) are commonplace, in generating sources and also in simply accessing moments sequentially. This class builds a vector of \((l,m)\) pairs that can be used in a single loop over all flux moments \(\Phi^m_l\),

\[ \Psi(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{\Omega}) \equiv \sum^M_{i=0} Y_{i}(\mathbf{r}) \Phi_{i}(\mathbf{r}) \]

where the sumation is actually done using the re-indexing \( i = \text{ind}(m,l) \) and the convention

\begin{align} & Y_{i}(\mathbf{r}) := Y_{l}^{m}(\mathbf{r}) \\ & \Phi_{i}(\mathbf{r}) := \Phi_{l}^{m}(\mathbf{r}) . \end{align}

For 3-d, the moments are ordered as

\[ [(0,0)] \, , \, \, \, [(1,-1),\, (1,0)\, (1,1)] \, , \, \, \, [(2,-2),\,(2,-1)\, \ldots \]

For an angular order of \(L\), there are \((L+1)^2\) moments.

For 2-d, several moments can be eliminated by symmetry. Only those moments for which \(l+m\) is even are retained (see Hebert). Thus, the moments are ordered as

\[ [(0,0)] \, , \, \, \, [(1,-1),\, (1,1)] \, , \, \, \, [(2,-2),\,(2,0)\, \ldots \]

For an angular order of \(L\), there are \((L+1)(L+2)/2\) moments.

For 1-d, \(m=0\), so that the moments are ordered by \(l\) alone, from 0 to the given order. Hence, there are \(L+1\) moments.

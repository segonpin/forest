Detailed Description

template<int dim>
class Forest::MomentsToDirections< dim >

Moments to directions class.

Given a moment expansion of the angular flux in spherical harmonics, we project it into a discrete ordinates representation of the angular flux,

\begin{align*} \Psi (\mathbf{r}) = \mathcal{M} \Phi(\mathbf{r}) \end{align*}

where the angular flux is a vector containing all the directions, and the moments flux is a vector containing all the moments, i.e.,

\begin{align*} & \Psi (\mathbf{r}) \equiv \left[ \begin{array}{c} \Psi_{0} (\mathbf{r}) \\ \vdots \\ \Psi_{n} (\mathbf{r}) \\ \vdots \\ \Psi_{N-1} (\mathbf{r}) \\ \end{array} \right], \quad & \Phi(\mathbf{r}) \equiv \left[ \begin{array}{c} \Phi_{0}^{0} (\mathbf{r}) \\ \vdots \\ \Phi_{l}^{m} (\mathbf{r}) \\ \vdots \\ \Phi_{L}^{L} (\mathbf{r}) \\ \end{array} \right], \quad \end{align*}

And the operator \( M \) is defined as obtaining the projection for a particular direction as follows:

\begin{align*} \Psi_n (\mathbf{r}) = \mathcal{M}_{n} \Phi(\mathbf{r}) & = \sum_{l = 0}^{L}\sum_{m=-l}^{l} Y_{l}^{m}(\Omega_n) \Phi_{l}^{m}(\mathbf{r}) \\ & = \sum_{i = 0}^{M} Y_{i}(\Omega_n) \Phi_{i}(\mathbf{r}) & n = 0, \dots, N-1 . \end{align*}

In practice we do not want to deal with two indices for the spherical harmonics, so that a unique index is defined by the MomentIndexer class.

Note
This operator acts independently of the energy group. Thus, when we want to apply this operation to the vector composed by all the energy groups, the operator should be applied independently to each group.

It applies \(\mathcal{M}_{i \to n}\) to the \( i\)-moment.

It returns the vector \( \Psi_n (\mathbf{r}) \) calculated by

\begin{align*} \Psi_n (\mathbf{r}) & = \Psi_n + \mathcal{M}_{i \to n} \Phi_{i}(\mathbf{r}) \\ & = \Psi_n + Y_{i}(\Omega_n) \Phi_{i}(\mathbf{r}) \end{align*}

where \( i = \text{index}(m,l) \) is the moment indexer function to align all indices in a vector to use just one loop.

Note
For the zero order moment we perform a direct assignment independent of the particular direction

\begin{align*} \Psi_n (\mathbf{r}) = \mathcal{M}_{0 \to n} \Phi_{0}(\mathbf{r}) = \Phi_{0}(\mathbf{r}) \end{align*}

It applies \(\mathcal{M}_{0 \to n}\) to the \( 0\)-moment.

It returns the vector \( \Psi_n (\mathbf{r}) \) calculated by

\begin{align*} \Psi_n (\mathbf{r}) = \mathcal{M}_{0 \to n} \Phi_{0}(\mathbf{r}) = \Phi_{0}(\mathbf{r}) \end{align*}

where \( \Phi_{0} \) is the scalar flux, and then the zero order spherical harmonic function is a constant equal to 1, and we have the angular representation independent of the particular direction.
